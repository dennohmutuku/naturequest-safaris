Best Time to Witness the Great Migration

June 15, 2024 John Smith Wildlife 12 Comments

The Great Migration is one of nature's most spectacular events, with over two million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles making their annual journey across the Serengeti-Mara ecosystem.

Understanding the Migration Cycle

The Great Migration is not a single event but a year-round movement of wildlife following the rains and fresh grass. Here's a month-by-month breakdown:

December to March

During these months, the herds are in the southern Serengeti, where the grass is rich and nutritious. This is also calving season, with thousands of wildebeest calves born each day.

April to June

As the rains end and the grass begins to dry, the herds start their journey northward through the central and western Serengeti.

July to October

This is often considered the highlight of the migration, when the herds cross the Mara River between Tanzania and Kenya. The dramatic river crossings are a favorite among photographers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Best Time to Visit

While the migration is a year-round event, certain times offer unique experiences:

  • February: Witness the calving season in the southern Serengeti
  • July to October: See the dramatic river crossings
  • November: Watch the herds return south

Travel Tips

  1. Book your safari well in advance, especially for peak season (July-October)
  2. Stay at least 3-4 days to increase your chances of seeing a river crossing
  3. Choose mobile camps that follow the migration for the best experience
  4. Bring appropriate camera gear to capture the action
